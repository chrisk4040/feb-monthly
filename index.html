George Washington

George Washington was the first President of the United States, serving two terms from 1789 to 1797. Born on February 22, 1732, in Westmoreland County, Virginia, he played a crucial role in the founding of the nation. Washington is often referred to as the "Father of His Country" for his leadership during the American Revolutionary War and his influence in the drafting of the Constitution. He passed away on December 14, 1799, leaving behind a legacy of integrity and dedication to the principles of democracy.
